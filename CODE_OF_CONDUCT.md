# Code of Conduct

## Our Standards

`reportilo` is a health research software project. We expect
contributors and users to keep discussion professional, evidence-based,
and respectful.

Examples of behavior that helps the project:

- Use welcoming and inclusive language.
- Focus criticism on code, data, documentation, and methods, not people.
- State uncertainty clearly when discussing reporting guidance or data
  quality.
- Respect different levels of experience with R, Shiny, React, and
  research reporting guidelines.
- Accept maintainers’ decisions about scope, release readiness, and
  moderation.

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Harassment, threats, insults, or discriminatory language.
- Sexualized language or imagery.
- Personal attacks, trolling, or sustained disruption.
- Public or private disclosure of another person’s private information.
- Knowingly misleading claims about guideline coverage, checklist
  validation, or research reporting standards.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies in all project spaces, including GitHub
issues, pull requests, discussions, code review, documentation, and any
public or private communication about the project.

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reported to the project
maintainer at the email address listed in `DESCRIPTION`. Reports will be
reviewed as promptly and confidentially as practical.

Maintainers may remove comments, close issues, reject contributions, or
block participants when needed to protect the project and its community.
